Motivation

     Poland started the Family 500+ program to provide families benefits in an effort to fight high levels of childhood poverty. Families receive 500PLN per month for each child after the first until the child’s 18th birthday. The program was announced in early-mid 2015 and went into effect April 2016. At the same time, Poland has been experiencing a declining birth rate for many years and the Family 500+ program hoped to encourage fertility. I decided to focus on what effects this program has had on the country’s birth rate.

Narrative Visualization

     The hybrid narrative structure I used for this visualization is Interactive Slideshow. I am using this structure to tell a short story regarding two areas of investigation. It is organized with an introduction, an "About" slide the viewer can navigate down to. Navigating right brings the viewer to the first slide where they navigate through chart scenes to see the story I am presenting as well as and further explore data independently. The viewer then navigates right to the next slide where they view the second chart's scenes for the story and individual data exploration. Arrows in the lower right hand corner navigate this Interactive Slideshow.

Scenes

     The stories in the slides are told in a consistent manner throuh the use of annotations on each scene in addition to the detailed info on the side. The viewer can choose to step through the scenes in any order based on the scene numbers in the buttons at the top of the graph. Following the natural progression through the numbered scenes, viewers are guided through the information I chose to highlight.
     The first slide consists of multiple scenes covering the total births over the past decade as well as births by birth order. The viewer can choose to step through the scenes in any order based on the scene numbers in the buttons at the top of the graph, and following those scenes in order tells the story of the overall decreasing birth rate in the country until the start of the Family 500 program, after which overall birth rate increases. The viewer then learns that the births of second and third children increase greatly, which are the children targeted by the program, indicating a measurable level of success. The final scene in this slide allows the viewer to interact with and explore the data on their own with zoom, toggling lines on or off, click-drag to pan data.
     The viewer can then transition the presentation to the next slide where they are presented with an investigation on the age of mothers when they gave birth. It is a stacked bar chart with each years' data being a stack of births broken down by marital status and location, grouped by mother's age. This identifies the demographics most affected by the introduction of the program. This follows the same scene presentation as the first slide by providing scene navigation buttons at the top of the chart. It also follows the consistent annotation structure to tell the story across scenes following the natural progression of the numbered slides. The viewer is told the story on decreasing or slowing birth rate, especially in younger mothers, until the start of the program when birth rate goes up across all age groups, espcially older mothers. The viewer can explore the full data at the final scene.

Annotations

     Annotations are used in the scenes to highlight meaningful elements of the data in the charts. They follow a consistent structure across slides as well as scenes within the slides. Changing the scene through the use of the numbered scene buttons will provide a new, different view of the data with different annotations providing information for that scene. In addition to the static annotations on each scene, viewers may hover over data elements to get tooltips with exact data for the point in time or category they wish to investigate.

Parameters

     The main parameter in each slide is the scene number provided in the buttons. These scene number change the years for which data is provided, so year is partially a parameter. Clicking the scene parameter buttons will update the view to provide the chart with essentially the "page" of the story so that the story can be told. Stepping through the pages will highlight information with annotations as the text of each page's story. In the first slide's final chart scene, parameters are the birth order categories of Total births, 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, or 5th+ child. Toggling these parameters will filter their respective data on or off by the viewer and provide the viewer control of the data for investigation.

Triggers

     As this is an interactive slideshow, triggers are used with the reveal.js arrows in the lower right hand corner for transitioning the slides. These arrows highlight on hover to indicate they are interactive and appear only on valid transitions to drive the presentation. The numbered scene buttons in each slide drive the changes the for the charts. They highlight on hover to indicate they are interactive. Current scene button is grayed out to indicate position.
     The first chart on total births and birth order has triggers for data filtering. Clicking items in the legend will enable/disable birth order category lines in the chart which not only toggle the lines to filter the data, but they also rescale the axes for only the currently enabled data. These instructions are labeled beneath the legend to inform the viewer of the control. The viewer can zoom in on data with mouse scroll to see more detail. The user can also click-drag the chart to pan across the data. Hovering over lines will trigger a tooltip with more information about the year in the mouse hover range. Additionally, this hover trigger draws circles over the data points for which more data is being displayed in the tooltip.
     The second chart has less interactivity than the first, but still provides methods for further investigation of the data by the viewer. Hovering over bar segments in the stacked bar chart triggers a tooltip which provides more information for the year hovered in the current age group chart. It provides the same slide and scene transition triggers as well.

Births in Poland by Birth Order

This graph focuses on the number of births per year while also paying investigating the birth order of the child. This is important to look at because the Family 500+ program’s financial benefits are given for each child after the first.

Click through the scene buttons at the upper left hand of the chart to step through the story. You will learn how Poland’s birth rate had been decreasing for the majority of the previous decade. The government identified that poverty levels were a large contributing factor to this downward trend, so they studied the potential impact of providing financial incentives to families with multiple children. You will learn that in the first couple of years after the announcement and implementation of the program the birth rate reversed course and increased rather significantly. While not many mothers started new families (flat rate for first children) man existing families added second and third children.

Once the final scene is reached you are free to explore the data closer. A tooltip will provide instruction on navigating the data, plus they will remain as a footnote below the chart.
Births in Poland by Mother's Age

This chart explores who is having children with respect to the mothers’ age. A demographic breakout is used for mother’s marital status and location of birth (rural vs urban). The cumulative total of each demographics’ births account for the total of births for each age group for the year. Each discrete demographic is represented by a different color in order to highlight the different demographics.

Click through the scene buttons at the upper left hand of the chart to step through the story. You will see that there is an ongoing trend where younger mothers are having significantly fewer children year-over-year. Older mothers are having more children year-over-year, but the absolute number of children being born to these age groups does not make up for the losses in the younger age groups. In all age groups, the number of children born to unmarried women stays fairly flat.

Continuing through the story you will learn that once the Family 500+ program is announced and implemented, the decrease in births to younger age groups slows down and goes flat. Older mothers continue having more children during this time as well. The most significant increases in births are in the middle age groups. These are the women that were expected to see the most benefit from the government stipends and the data appears to mirror that.
